Perks of being a member

At BIB, our core belief revolves around absolute transparency in every facet of our business. We take pride in delivering thorough and comprehensive reporting to every startup that joins our ranks. This unwavering commitment serves as a robust defense against any potential fraud or malicious activities that could potentially arise. By upholding this value of transparency, we're dedicated to fostering an atmosphere of trust and credibility. This approach ensures that we maintain the utmost ethical standards and accountability throughout our operations.

What you get

  1. Business Plan Analysis Evaluate the startup's business plan, including its mission, value proposition, target market, and competitive landscape.
  2. Financial Analysis: Scrutinize financial statements, projections, and historical performance to assess the startup's financial health, revenue streams, profitability, and cash flow.
  3. Market Analysis: Analyze the startup's market size, trends, growth potential, competitive positioning, and barriers to entry.
  4. Product or Service Assessment: Understand the startup's product or service, its uniqueness, features, benefits, and how it addresses customer pain points.
  5. Intellectual Property (IP) Check: Verify the startup's intellectual property portfolio, including patents, trademarks, copyrights, and any potential legal disputes.
  6. Team Evaluation: Evaluate the startup's management team, their expertise, experience, and track record in building and scaling businesses.
  7. Legal and Compliance Review: Assess the startup's legal structure, contracts, licenses, permits, regulatory compliance, and potential legal liabilities.
  8. Customer Validation: Talk to existing customers or users to validate the startup's value proposition, product-market fit, and gather feedback.
  9. Technology and Product Development: Review the technology stack, development roadmap, and assess the scalability and sustainability of the product.
  10. Operational Assessment: Understand the startup's operational processes, supply chain, distribution, and any potential bottlenecks.
  11. Market Traction: Evaluate the startup's market traction, user adoption, revenue growth, and customer retention.
  12. Competitor Analysis: Analyze key competitors, their strengths, weaknesses, and how the startup differentiates itself in the market.
  13. Financial Projections Validation: Verify the assumptions and logic behind the startup's financial projections, ensuring they are realistic and achievable.
  14. Risk Assessment: Identify and assess potential risks and challenges the startup may face and how they plan to mitigate them.
  15. Exit Strategy: Understand the startup's long-term plans, potential exit opportunities, and strategies for providing returns to investors.
  16. Reference Checks: Contact references, partners, or clients mentioned by the startup to validate claims and gather insights.
  17. Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Considerations: Evaluate the startup's commitment to ethical practices, sustainability, and social responsibility.
  18. Due Diligence Report: Compile all findings into a comprehensive due diligence report for potential investors, partners, or stakeholders.
